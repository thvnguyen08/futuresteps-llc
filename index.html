Breaking Immigration News

Green Card Update

DOL Regulatory Agenda Signals Overhaul of PERM Labor Certification Process

The Department of Labor's newly released regulatory agenda shows plans to modernize the PERM labor certification process used by most employment-based green card sponsors, with rules that haven't been substantially updated since 2004. Proposed changes include stricter recruitment and documentation requirements, which immigration attorneys warn could lengthen an already lengthy process for employers and applicants alike.

Jul 6, 2026 Source: Newsweek
Enforcement Update

ICE Arrests Surge to 10,000 in Five Days as Deportation Push Widens Nationwide

Immigration and Customs Enforcement arrested more than 10,000 people over a five-day span in late June — roughly 2,000 arrests per day — as the agency shifted from high-profile city sweeps to quieter nationwide enforcement through check-ins, traffic stops, and street arrests. ICE detention populations climbed to about 39,000 in June, the highest level of the year.

Jul 2, 2026 Source: AP / U.S. News
Asylum Processing

USCIS Opens New Asylum Office in Atlanta to Expand Interview Capacity

USCIS announced a new Atlanta Asylum Office covering Georgia and Alabama, with affirmative asylum interviews beginning July 8, 2026 across three temporary locations — a new Atlanta site, the Atlanta Field Office, and the Montgomery, Alabama Field Office. A permanent office location is expected in 2027.

Jul 1, 2026 Source: USCIS.gov
